Privacy Policy

Last updated: 21 June 2026. Plum is pre-launch — this policy describes how Plum handles data for merchants who connect their store.

Plum is an analytics app for Shopify. For the data inside your store, Plum acts as a data processor on your behalf: you are the controller of your customers' personal data, and Plum processes it only to provide the analytics you ask for. We wrote this the same way we built the product — plainly, with no claims we can't stand behind.

Who we are

Plum (plum-labs.com). Privacy contact: vladimirvukojicic@gmail.com. A human reads every message.

What we access and process

  • Shopify store data you authorize — orders, products, and customer records, read through the Shopify API under the scopes you approve when you install the app. This can include personal data about your customers (names, contact details, order history) that lives in your store.
  • Account & install details — your store domain, the access token issued by Shopify at install, and your contact email.
  • Billing — handled entirely through Shopify's Billing API. We see your subscription status, not your card details.
  • Operational logs — requests, errors, and timestamps needed to run, secure, and debug the service.

How we use it

  • To build your governed metrics and answer your questions in the app, over the MCP connection, and in connected BI tools.
  • To operate, secure, support, and improve the service.
  • To bill you for the plan you chose (via Shopify).

We do not sell your data, and we do not use one merchant's data to serve another.

AI processing (the honest detail)

Plum's Ask feature uses a third-party AI provider (Anthropic) to interpret your question and phrase the answer. The model never receives your database credentials and does not author queries; the governed metric and its SQL live in Plum. To produce a written answer, the results of a governed query (the numbers and rows behind that answer) may be sent to Anthropic's API. Anthropic processes this under its commercial API terms and does not use it to train its models. We will tell you here before that materially changes.

Sub-processors

  • Shopify — the platform your store and billing run on.
  • Anthropic — the AI provider behind the Ask feature.
  • Our cloud hosting and database provider — where the service and your warehouse run.

We keep this list current and will note material changes on this page.

How long we keep it

We retain your store data while the app is installed. When you uninstall, Shopify sends a shop/redact request and Plum deprovisions your tenant and deletes the warehouse built from your store. When a customer exercises a deletion right, Shopify sends customers/redact, which we honor; because your analytics are rebuilt from your store, a customer removed at the source falls out of Plum on the next sync.

Your rights — and your customers' rights

Depending on where you and your customers live (e.g. GDPR, CCPA), you may have rights to access, correct, export, or delete personal data. Requests from your customers flow through Shopify's mandatory privacy webhooks (customers/data_request, customers/redact, shop/redact), which Plum implements. For your own account data, email us and we'll act on verified requests.

Security

We limit access to your data and credentials to what's needed to run the service, use a read-only, least-privilege database role for analytics and BI connections, and verify the authenticity of every Shopify webhook before acting on it. We continue to strengthen our safeguards as the service moves to production infrastructure. No method of storage or transmission is perfectly secure, and we won't claim otherwise.

Cookies & tracking

This marketing site uses no advertising or tracking cookies. The app uses only the essential cookies and authentication needed to keep you signed in.

Children

Plum is a business tool and is not directed to children.

Changes to this policy

We'll post any changes here and update the date above; material changes will be called out.
